Caclulus 3 Review:

3-Dimensional Vectors: These are to put simply a short line with a magnitude length and direction towards a particular point. It can be referenced in multiple dimensions that includes further than 3-D like 4-D but can't be visualized beyond 3-D Space.

Three ways to look at vectors: As described by a famous Math youtube 3Blue1Brown
According to the video he has said a physics, computer science, and mathmatics student looks at vectors differently. Physics students look at them as points in space with a direction and magnitude in a form of it pointing towards a point given x and y components. A computer science student usually calls vectors another name called Arrays which is basically a set of ordered lists containing data that can be redistributed and changed by adding or taking data away from it. A mathematics student looks at it in both ways and in more abstract ways of the nature of vectors and how they work.

Vectors: They come in a "n" number of components depending of the dimension. A three dimensional vector has three components being X,Y, and Z. They are all in part of a vector that is named by a variable with an arrow on top. In engineering/unit vector notation, they are represented respectively as and
V = < X, Y, Z >.
Engineering/Unit Vector Notation: The x component is the symbol i-hat = < 1, 0, 0 > while the y component is j-hat = < 0, 1, 0 > and the z component is k-hat = < 0, 0, 1>.

Component Angles: The angle of the x component is alpha while the angle of the y component is beta and the z component is gamma.

Magnitude and Direction: The main ways of describing vectors are magnitude which are the length of the vectors and caluclated using the formula ||r||=√(x² + y² + z²). It describes force, speed, and so on. Direction can be described by a unit vector and it's scaler which acts as a multiplier or factored number from the intial vector.

Dot-Product: It is a scaler that represent a relationship between vectors in several ways and is represent as V*W which each vector having components multiply each other's respective component and adding them together. Firstly, it can tell if two 3-D vectors are orthogonal (three dimensional version of perpendicular) then the dot product of those two vectors = 0.
